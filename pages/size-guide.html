Eyewear Size Guide

Find the perfect fit for your face

How to Find Your Frame Size

Frame measurements are usually printed on the inside of the temple arm. Here's how to read them:

Lens Width (Eye Size)

The horizontal width of each lens, measured in millimeters. Typical range: 40-62mm

Bridge Width

The distance between the two lenses at the bridge. Typical range: 14-24mm

Temple Length (Arm Length)

The length of the arms from hinge to tip. Typical range: 120-150mm

Find Your Face Shape

Different frame styles complement different face shapes. Find your perfect match!

Oval

Balanced proportions with slightly wider cheekbones

Best frames: Almost any style! You're lucky - try square, rectangular, or geometric frames.

Round

Similar width and length with soft angles

Best frames: Angular, rectangular frames to add definition. Avoid round frames.

Square

Strong jawline with equal width forehead

Best frames: Round or oval frames to soften angles. Cat-eye styles work great too!

Heart

Wider forehead tapering to narrow chin

Best frames: Bottom-heavy frames, round shapes, or light-colored frames that add width to lower face.

Oblong

Longer than wide with straight cheeks

Best frames: Oversized frames, decorative temples, or frames with depth to shorten the face.

Diamond

Narrow forehead and jaw with wide cheekbones

Best frames: Cat-eye, oval, or rimless frames that highlight your cheekbones.

Pro Tips for the Perfect Fit

Check Your Current Glasses

Look at the inside of your temple arm for numbers like "52-18-140" which represent lens width, bridge width, and temple length.

Measure Frame Width

The total frame width should match your face width. Frames shouldn't extend past your temples or feel too tight.

Check Pupil Position

Your pupils should be centered in the lenses. If they're too far to the side, the frame is too wide or narrow.

Temple Fit

Temples should fit comfortably over your ears without pressing or sliding. Adjust by gently bending if needed.

Bridge Comfort

The bridge should rest comfortably on your nose without pinching or sliding down. Look for adjustable nose pads if needed.
